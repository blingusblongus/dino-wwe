# 413 Days Later: A WDAA Season 1 Preview

Four hundred and thirteen days ago, Rex Kingston pinned the Apex
Championship to his shoulder and walked out of Tar Pit Arena without a
scratch. He's been wearing that belt for longer than most marriages
survive. He's also been defending it against a murderer's row of dinos
who keep forgetting to win.

This Thursday, the WDAA returns for Season 1, Episode 1. I want to
walk you through what's actually at stake, because if you've only been
half-watching since December, you've missed a lot.

## The Champion

**Rex Kingston** is who he is. His father is the Emperor. His last
three defenses all ended in round 2. He has not been pushed past round
four since November of the year before last. Whatever you think of the
Kingston name — and after fifteen years of Apex title scandals, you
should think *something* — the guy can finish a match.

The question is whether he can still finish Anky Bronson. Because
Anky is not built to be finished.

## The Challenger

**Anky Bronson** won a six-dino gauntlet three weeks ago to earn this
shot. The Emperor called it, quote, "a rigged charity drive." Anky
responded by *not responding*, which is his whole thing. The man taped
his own wrists at his own gauntlet match. He brought his own water. He
took home the trophy and drove his own truck back to the warehouse
district where he lives, alone, in an apartment over a laundromat.

His father worked security for the Emperor for thirty years and got
stiffed on the pension. That's the whole story. That's why he's here.
If you need more motivation than that, you're watching the wrong sport.

## The Wildcards

**Velo Machado** is livid about not getting this title shot, and he's
told anyone with a microphone. He's on the opener against Dilo
DeVille. I expect him to win ugly.

**Dilo DeVille** hasn't taken a match in six weeks. He's been in the
parking lot three separate times having what the cameras described as
"animated conversations" with the Emperor. Dr. Mendoza has been seen
carrying a briefcase that multiple insiders swear is identical to the
Emperor's briefcase. I have no idea what that means. Nobody does. That
appears to be the point.

## What I'm Watching For

1. **Round count.** If Rex can't put Anky away in three, the reign is
   already over — he just doesn't know it yet.
2. **The Emperor's body language.** Watch him during the opener. He
   doesn't usually watch undercards. If he's watching this one, we
   know something.
3. **Dilo at ringside.** I don't think he's on commentary. I don't
   think he's in the main event. So where is he?

Tar Pit Arena, Thursday, 8 PM. No excuses for missing this one.
