# The Kingston Dynasty Cracks: Episode 1 Recap

Rex Kingston is still the Apex Champion. Write that down. Underline it.
Now draw a question mark next to it, because everything else about
Thursday night suggests a reign that's running on fumes.

## The Cold Open

The show opened backstage with a cold open so quiet you could hear
Anky Bronson's wrist tape. Rex walked up with the Emperor. Condescended.
Waved the belt in Anky's face, then yanked it back when Anky reached
for it.

*"You'll touch this belt Friday. Not before."*

That's the Emperor talking to a man who has never once, in the
five-year history of this company, been afraid of him. Anky didn't
respond. He didn't need to. You could see it in his mouth when the
belt got close — he *almost* lunged. If the Emperor had been half a
second slower, we would have had a different show.

## Match 1: Dilo Over Velo, Close Decision

Velo Machado came into the opener carrying two weeks of public fury
about the gauntlet. He left the ring carrying the first loss of his
WDAA career. **Dilo DeVille** took it by decision, 87.6 to 83.3,
without needing Dr. Mendoza to step a toe onto the apron.

The story of the match was round three. Velo had been cycling through
his Pack Attack setups. Dilo was countering every fourth strike.
Starting at the three-minute mark, Dilo was countering every *second*
strike. Somebody in the production truck noticed; the cameras started
cutting to Mendoza in the corner. Mendoza was checking his watch.

This is a long way of saying that Dilo did something mid-match to
change his speed, and the WDAA should probably look into whether
"something" should be legal. (It will not look into it.)

## The Briefcase Bit

Between matches, the Emperor walked into Dilo's locker room. Door
closed. We stayed outside. We heard a briefcase open. We heard a
whistle. We heard the briefcase close. We did not cut to the contents.
**Remember that.**

## Main Event: Rex Retains (By Decision)

For the first time in 413 days, Rex Kingston could not finish his
challenger. He could not even come close to finishing his challenger.
Anky Bronson ate the opening Dynastic Chomp and stayed *standing*.
Nobody eats that move and stays standing. The arena made a sound I
have not heard before. I think it was surprise.

Round four delivered the moment the whole show was building toward —
and then *didn't deliver it*. Dilo DeVille appeared at the top of the
ramp holding a briefcase. The Emperor gestured for him to come down.
Dilo stared. Smiled. **Turned around and walked back through the
curtain.**

Rex was visibly shaken for the rest of the match. He never hit the
Extinction Event setup. Anky got to the seventh round — the seventh
round, with Rex Kingston, a thing nobody has survived in two years —
and let the judges do their job.

**Final:** Rex Kingston retains by decision, 90.2 to 84.9.

## The Closer

Parking lot. The Emperor screaming at Dilo. Dilo standing next to a
limousine with **two identical briefcases** on the roof. "This one's
the payment. This one's the evidence." He took both briefcases into
the limo and drove off.

Somewhere in that limo is a stack of documents with the Kingston name
on it. We do not know how thick the stack is. We know the Emperor is
worried about it.

## Stockton's Takeaway

Rex walked away with the belt. That is technically a successful
defense. In every other sense this was a loss. The Emperor's
insurance policy didn't activate. His bagman walked off with the
bag *and* the receipts. His son can't finish matches anymore.

Next week we should probably get a response from the Emperor. Or we
should probably get a reveal of what Dilo was actually paid for.

Or we should get both. And god help everyone in the Kingston camp if
we do.
