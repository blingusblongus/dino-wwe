# The Parking Lot Problem

*Editor's column*

I've been covering this company for six years and I have never been
more certain that we are about to see a regime change.

Thursday night, the Emperor lost his leverage. He lost it publicly. He
lost it to a dinosaur who, nine months ago, was not being booked on
broadcast television. That is a remarkable thing, and I want to dwell
on it for a minute, because I do not think the community has fully
metabolized what happened.

## What we saw

The mid-show vignette: the Emperor enters Dilo DeVille's locker room
carrying a briefcase. The door closes. When the Emperor emerges, he is
noticeably lighter. When Dilo emerges, for the main event, he is
carrying a briefcase. Not *a* briefcase. **A second** briefcase. He
told us, in the parking lot, that the first one contains the money he
was paid. He told us the second one contains "the evidence."

He told us all of this *on camera*. He told us all of this **while the
Emperor was standing in front of him**.

The Emperor did not take the briefcases. The Emperor could not take
the briefcases. Because taking them would have confirmed, to a bank of
cameras, that the briefcases were his.

He stood in a parking lot and watched a Dilophosaurus drive away with
the only thing that has kept the Kingston name safe for twenty years.

## What it means

For the last decade, the Apex Championship has traveled down a very
short list of names: Kingston, Rex Sr. Kingston, Rex Jr. A brief
interregnum in 2019 when the strap was held by a Carnotaurus whose
contract, the Emperor would later claim, was "administratively
lapsed."

That is not a wrestling company. That is a family business with a
venue.

What Dilo has in that second briefcase is not a stack of photographs.
It is the end of the family business. The photographs, we can
assume, contain enough evidence of fixed matches, paid referees, and
engineered title runs to void fifteen years of results. The
Commission — whoever and whatever the Commission is; Dilo referenced
it in passing, and the WDAA has not named it before — would have to
vacate titles. It would have to vacate *rankings*. It would, in the
extreme, have to vacate the company itself.

The Emperor knows this. Dilo knows the Emperor knows this. The
Emperor knows Dilo knows he knows this. We are in a Cold War. And the
Cold War has a champion.

## What comes next

Three scenarios. I rank them in order of likelihood:

1. **The Emperor tries to buy it back.** He will fail. Dilo does not
   need money — he has the money, *and* the leverage, *and* now a
   belt. There is nothing the Emperor can offer him.

2. **The Emperor burns it down.** He leaks the photographs himself,
   through a third party, and survives the blast. This is what the
   Emperor would have done ten years ago. I am not sure he has it in
   him anymore.

3. **Rex breaks.** This is the scenario I am actually watching for,
   because it's the one the stat sheet suggests. Rex went seven
   rounds with Anky Bronson for the first time in his career, and he
   did it without a finisher. The confidence is cracked. If the
   Emperor abandons him — and everything I'm hearing suggests the
   Emperor is already drafting that letter — then Rex is a 420-day
   champion who will spend the rest of Season 1 discovering what it's
   like to lose.

Buckle up. I don't think we're getting a title change next week. I
think we're getting *several*.

*— Kressick*
