# 420 Days, Gone in Seven Rounds: Episode 2 Recap

Dilo DeVille is the new WDAA Apex Champion. I'll give you a minute to
sit with that sentence. I had to sit with it for about forty minutes
before I could start typing.

## The Cold Open That Changed Everything

Dilo opened the show sitting on a folding chair at the top of the
entrance ramp, a briefcase across his knees, no music, no pyro, a
single spotlight. He opened the briefcase. He held a photograph up to
the camera.

It was the Emperor, circa 1991, handing a referee an envelope in a
parking lot.

He held up another. And another. And another. He named years. He
named opponents. He named matches that people in my business have
whispered about for twenty years. He named **King Rex Sr.**

He said he had already sent copies to "the Commission" — whatever the
Commission is; nobody on the WDAA broadcast has *ever* said that word
on camera before — and would review them in the morning. Unless —

Unless the Emperor came out right now and booked him against Rex for
the Apex Championship. Tonight. No interference. No ringside.

The Emperor walked out. Said it flatly. Did not even enter the ring.
"Tonight's main event is Rex Kingston versus Dilo DeVille. For the
WDAA Apex Championship. I will not be at ringside. That's all."

He dropped the microphone. He walked back through the curtain.

I will tell you what I felt in that moment. I felt sorry for him. And
I have hated this man for six years.

## The Contender's Match

With the main event resettled, the Emperor announced — in a second
statement that had to have been scripted in the ten minutes between
the cold open and the opener — that next week's PPV would feature
Anky Bronson versus Velo Machado for the right to face the champion.

Anky won. Dominantly. 94.8 to 79.5 by decision.

I will not spend many words on this match because Anky did not spend
many strikes. He walked Velo into the corner, he *stood there*, and he
let Velo break himself against the armor. The Blue-Collar Barrage
landed at full speed for the first time ever. The crowd chanted
ANKY — actually chanted, actually *in rhythm* — and Anky never went
for the finisher. He didn't need to. He was making a point.

**The point was: "I am still the wall."**

Velo did not leave the ring for a very long time after the bell.

## Rex, Alone

Between the contender's match and the main event, we got one of the
strangest vignettes the WDAA has ever aired. Rex Kingston, in his
locker room, by himself, with no Emperor and no entourage. He took a
selfie with the Apex Championship on his shoulder. He looked at the
picture. **He did not post it.** He put the phone face-down.

That is the image I cannot get out of my head from Thursday. Not the
title change. That.

## The Main Event

Rex Kingston vs. Dilo DeVille. No Emperor. The first time in Rex's
career he has walked to the ring without his father.

The match was ugly from the bell. Dilo landed for six in round one.
For nearly eight in round two. For seven in round three. By round
five Rex was sub-65 HP and the commentators had stopped doing bits.
This was not a work. This was a *fight*, and Rex was losing it to a
dinosaur whose power rating is 5.

He clawed back in rounds six and seven. It wasn't enough. It was
never going to be enough. The judges confirmed what the crowd already
knew.

**Dilo DeVille def. Rex Kingston by decision, 70.6 to 62.2.**
**Rex's reign ends at 420 days.**

## The Stable

After the bell, Mendoza took the microphone. "On behalf of *our*
faction — I'd like to introduce the other two."

Red lights. New music (I've called around and nobody knows who
licensed it). Two silhouettes at the top of the ramp. One tall —
taller than anyone on the current roster. One small — small enough to
walk under the top rope without ducking.

They did not come to the ring. They stood at the top of the ramp.
They watched Dilo hold the belt above his head.

The broadcast faded to black on a shot of Anky Bronson, in a hallway,
taping his wrists, watching the monitor.

## Stockton's Takeaway

I have been doing this for a long time. I have never seen a
championship reign end this fast after looking this secure. Rex was a
lock two weeks ago. Two weeks ago I wrote a preview column that
treated his loss as a question of *when*, not *if* — and I still
thought the answer was at least a year away. I was wrong. I was
wrong by about eleven and a half months.

The PPV is in three weeks. Anky Bronson versus the new champion,
Dilo DeVille, for the Apex Championship. That was not the main event
anybody was booking a month ago. That is the main event we deserve.

And somewhere in this company, right now, there are two dinosaurs
nobody has named. We're about to find out who they are. We're going
to find out on the broadcast, on camera, whether the Emperor is ready
for it or not.

He is not ready for it.

*— Stockton*
