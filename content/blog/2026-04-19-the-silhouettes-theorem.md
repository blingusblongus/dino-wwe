# The Silhouettes Theorem: A Field Guide to What We Just Saw

*Disclaimer from the editors: PaleoPundit42 is a guest contributor.
These are his opinions. We are not confirming any of them. We are also
not denying any of them. Proceed at your own pace.*

---

Alright. You saw it. I saw it. Let's get scientific.

The Episode 2 closer gave us two silhouettes at the top of the ramp,
both explicitly members of Dilo DeVille's faction. The WDAA very
deliberately did not show us their faces. But the silhouettes
revealed *two specific things*:

- **Figure A (tall):** Taller than any dino on the current roster.
- **Figure B (small):** Small enough to walk under the top rope
  without ducking.

That is a very unusual pair. Most stables are built around similar
weight classes, or around a clear leader-and-two-muscle structure.
This is a **tall-small pairing**, the kind you would only book if the
two of them were telling a story *together*. I've been thinking about
what that story is. Here are seven theories.

## Theory 1: The Thumb Brothers *(45% confidence)*

You know the ones. **Big Thumb** and **Little Thumb.** Therizinosaurus
and Compsognathus respectively. They've been on the indies for
eighteen months working joint matches as "Two Thumbs Up" with a
character gimmick that's half circus act, half horror. Big Thumb has
a reach advantage that is basically uncounterable. Little Thumb is
too fast to see.

The WDAA has been scouting them for at least six months — I have a
source. The ramp silhouette heights match almost exactly.

**Why this is probably right:** The height ratio. The timing. The
gimmick fits Dilo's aesthetic (chaos + theater).

**Why this might be wrong:** The Thumb Brothers have never worked
heel before. The turn would need to be sold.

## Theory 2: The Lost Pack *(30% confidence)*

Velo Machado broke from his pack two years ago in a public falling
out. There were **two brothers** specifically named in the original
incident: an older, larger brother named *Kaiser* and a younger,
smaller one named *Notch*. They have not been seen on any circuit
since.

If the silhouettes are Kaiser and Notch, this changes everything.
Velo's entire arc this season recontextualizes. Dilo didn't just reject
him in the corridor backstage — **Dilo had Velo's brothers already
signed.** That rejection becomes the coldest move in professional
wrestling history, and Velo becomes a ticking time bomb.

**Why this is probably right:** It's the most emotionally satisfying
answer. It's the answer WWE would write.

**Why this might be wrong:** Kaiser and Notch are both Velociraptors,
which means roughly the same size class. The silhouette height gap is
too big.

## Theory 3: The Commission *(12% confidence)*

Wild card. Dilo name-dropped "the Commission" in his cold open. What
if the Commission isn't a regulatory body — what if it's a *faction*?
What if the two silhouettes are Commission enforcers who've been
working for Dilo the whole time?

**Why this is probably right:** It would explain why the Emperor
looked so visibly terrified when Dilo said the word. It would also
give us a 2026 equivalent of the NWO — an outside-authority invasion
storyline.

**Why this might be wrong:** It's a lot. It might be too much.

## Theory 4: The Emperor's Other Sons *(8% confidence)*

Okay, hear me out. There are long-standing rumors that Rex Kingston
has half-siblings from King Rex Sr.'s itinerant years. Nobody has
ever confirmed them. The Emperor's second briefcase — the one Dilo
*still hasn't opened on air* — could contain birth records. The
silhouettes could be **two Kingston sons** Rex has never met.

If this is right, the PPV booking becomes a family reunion fought at
full contact, and I will have to leave my job to write about it
full-time.

**Why this is probably right:** It doesn't need to be right. It's
just fun.

**Why this might be wrong:** Height gap doesn't check.

## Theories 5–7

- **Theory 5:** Two indie debutantes Dilo signed with the Emperor's
  money. No narrative arc, just a flex. *(3%)*
- **Theory 6:** One of them is Dr. Mendoza out of costume, revealing
  that Mendoza has been a wrestler the whole time. *(1%)*
- **Theory 7:** Anky Bronson. In disguise. Working a deep con on
  Dilo. *(1%, but my god, imagine.)*

## What to Watch For in Episode 3

Three tells that will narrow this down:

1. **Whether the new members get individual entrance music.** If
   they're getting music, they're getting a feud. If they're silent
   for the whole episode, they're muscle. Muscle rules out the Lost
   Pack theory.
2. **Whether Velo Machado addresses them on camera.** If he does, and
   he knows their names, it's the Lost Pack and I am throwing a
   party. If he doesn't — if he reacts with confusion or rage —
   we're in Thumb Brothers territory.
3. **Whether the Emperor *disappears*.** If he's off the show
   entirely in Episode 3, it's the Commission. If he's trying to
   negotiate with the new members directly, it's the Kingston sons.

I'll be watching. I won't sleep.
