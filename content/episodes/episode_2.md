# WDAA Episode 2 — "Evidence"

**Air date:** Season 1, Week 2
**Venue:** The Tar Pit Arena, Los Ángeles-Cretáceos
**Card:** #1 Contender's match (Anky vs. Velo); impromptu title defense
(Rex vs. Dilo, forced by blackmail).

---

## Cold Open — 8:00 PM, Live to Camera

The broadcast opens on an empty ring. House lights down. Then a single
spotlight on the entrance stage: DILO DEVILLE, sitting on a folding
chair, a briefcase across his knees. No music. No pyro.

> **Dilo:** Good evening. *(beat)* I promised you evidence.

He opens the briefcase. Lifts out a stack of photographs the size of a
phone book. Holds one up to the camera. A grainy photo of THE EMPEROR,
decades younger, handing a referee a manila envelope in a parking lot.

> **Dilo:** That's 1991. The Emperor versus Brutus Apatosaurus. The
> referee in that photograph *made fourteen bad calls in the main event
> that night*. I have his tax returns, too. He retired the next month.
> He bought a house.

Next photo. A judge. Next photo. A promoter. Next photo — **King Rex
Sr.** handing an envelope to a fighter we don't recognize, mid-1990s.

> **Dilo:** Every one of these pictures has a name. Every name has a
> match. Every match has a Kingston in it.

He puts the photographs back. Closes the briefcase.

> **Dilo:** I've already sent copies to the Commission. They'll review
> them in the morning. *Or* — *(pause)* — The Emperor can come out here
> right now and book me against his son for the Apex Championship.
> Tonight. No interference. No ringside. And we'll talk about the
> *second* briefcase some other week.

Camera holds on Dilo's face. Static crowd noise.

Then the Emperor's music hits.

---

## The Emperor's Response — 8:06 PM

THE EMPEROR walks to the ring. No entourage. No belt to carry — he hasn't
got one. He is visibly shaking. He doesn't enter the ring. He takes a
microphone from the timekeeper's table.

> **Emperor:** *(voice flat)* Tonight's main event is Rex Kingston
> versus Dilo DeVille. For the WDAA Apex Championship. I will not be at
> ringside. That's all.

He drops the mic. Walks back. The crowd doesn't know whether to cheer
or to be terrified. They settle on terrified.

> **🔧 Vignette effect:** Rex Confidence **-1** (evidence reveal shakes
> the dynasty). Dilo Confidence already capped.

---

## Backstage — 8:31 PM — The Alliance That Didn't Happen

VELO MACHADO corners DILO in a corridor near catering. Dilo is eating a
sandwich. Mendoza is five feet away pretending to check his phone.

> **Velo:** I'll cut to it. Whatever you're building — I want in. You
> need a second. You need muscle that can *read* a match. You need me.
> **Dilo:** *(chewing)* Do I.
> **Velo:** Look around. I'm the best technical fighter in this company
> and I'm stuck on undercards. You're the smartest heel in this company
> and you're about to run the whole thing. The math is obvious.
> **Dilo:** *(swallows)* Velo. I watched you lose to me last week, and
> I had a vial of cough syrup in my stomach and a migraine. You want
> in my stable, pal? Earn it. You haven't yet.

Dilo walks off with his sandwich. Mendoza follows without looking up
from his phone. Velo stands alone in the corridor for a long count.

> **🔧 Vignette effect:** Velo Motivation **+1** (cap; already maxed).
> Velo now legitimately dangerous. Also lonely.

---

## Anky's First Promo — 8:54 PM

The camera finds ANKY BRONSON by the locker room sinks. He is washing
his hands. He looks up. It is the first time in WDAA history he has
looked *directly* into a camera.

> **Anky:** I earned my shot.
> *(beat)*
> **Anky:** I'm going to keep it.

He turns the tap off. Walks off-camera.

The control room cuts to black for half a second because nobody had a
button ready for *Anky talking*.

> **🔧 Vignette effect:** Anky Confidence **+1** (8→9). Mic presence
> officially unlocked.

---

## Match 1 — Anky Bronson vs. Velo Machado
### #1 Contender's Match — Winner faces the Apex Champion at Paleozoic PPV

**The match:** It is a bloodbath disguised as a contender's match. Velo
is a technical wizard — he should be able to dance around Anky. He
can't. He's emotional. He's coming off two straight humiliations in
nine days and it shows in every strike selection. He goes for the Pack
Attack in round 1 and gets *walked into* by Anky like a car walking into
a wall.

Round 4: Anky lands the **Blue-Collar Barrage** at full speed for the
first time in his WDAA career. Velo eats every shot. Gets up. Eats them
again. The crowd starts chanting ANKY — not just cheering. **Chanting.**

Velo's reversals land in rounds 5 and 6 but they're desperation — he's
bleeding ring craft. Anky never goes for the Wrecking Ball. Doesn't
need to. He's not trying to finish Velo, he's trying to *send a message*,
and the message is: I am the wall that keeps standing.

> **Result:** **Anky Bronson def. Velo Machado by decision** (94.8 – 79.5).

Velo doesn't leave the ring for a long time after the bell. Nobody
comes to get him. Commentary notes, quietly, that this is his *second*
straight loss. He is 0-2 on the season. He was supposed to headline.

---

## Intermission Vignette — 9:37 PM — Rex Alone

The camera finds REX KINGSTON in his locker room. He is alone. There is
no Emperor. There is no entourage. There is a single mirror and Rex is
in front of it.

He opens his phone. Takes a selfie with the Apex Championship on his
shoulder.

Looks at the picture.

Puts the phone face-down on the counter without posting it.

The camera holds on the back of the phone for three seconds. It's
uncomfortable.

Cut to commercial.

> **🔧 Effect:** None mechanical. Narrative only. Rex has been alone for
> eleven minutes of show time. He has never been alone on a broadcast.

---

## Main Event — Rex Kingston (c) vs. Dilo DeVille
### WDAA Apex Championship — Emperor Barred From Ringside

**Pre-match:** No pre-match promos. Dilo walks out in his ring gear
carrying **both briefcases**. He sets them in his corner like pets.
Mendoza stays on the apron. Rex's music hits — nobody comes out for a
full twelve seconds. Then Rex appears. No Emperor with him. The
commentators note, audibly: *"I don't know if I've ever seen Rex walk
to a ring without his father."*

**The match:** It is *ugly* from the bell.

- **Round 1:** Dilo reverses Rex's opener, then lands a clean shot for
  nearly 6 damage. Crowd registers it: *Rex got rocked in round 1*.
- **Round 2:** Dilo lands for **7.6**. Rex staggers. The commentators
  have stopped doing bits. This is a real fight now.
- **Round 3:** Another 7.2 from Dilo. Rex is hurt. He is *actually
  hurt*. The Extinction Event setup is nowhere on his moveset tonight.
  He's throwing crowd-pleaser strikes. They're not working.
- **Round 4:** They trade. Rex gets a shot in for the first time that
  matters.
- **Round 5:** Dilo lands **7.7** — his biggest of the match. Rex HP
  below 65. He is on the verge of being finished by a dinosaur with a
  power rating of 5.
- **Rounds 6–7:** Rex finds his legs. Lands solid shots. Too little, too
  late. The clock is against him.

The bell rings. Both dinos are standing. The judges confer.

**The ring announcer, at the center of the ring:**
> "Ladies and gentlemen… the winner of this match — *(beat)* — AND
> **NEW WDAA APEX CHAMPION** —"

The crowd doesn't even let him finish.

> **Result:** **Dilo DeVille def. Rex Kingston by decision** (70.6 – 62.2).
> **Dilo DeVille is the new WDAA Apex Champion.**
> Rex Kingston's reign ends at **420 days**.

---

## Closing Segment — 10:58 PM

Dilo stands in the center of the ring with the belt. Rex sits on the
canvas, staring at nothing. The Emperor is nowhere. He isn't at
ringside. He isn't on the stage. He isn't on any monitor.

Mendoza takes the microphone.

> **Mendoza:** Ladies and gentlemen. On behalf of your new champion —
> and on behalf of *our* faction — I'd like to introduce the other two.

The lights go red. The music is new — we've never heard it before. Two
figures walk to the top of the ramp. They stop in silhouette. We never
see their faces. We see:

- One is **tall** — taller than any dino on the current roster.
- One is **small** — small enough to walk *under* the top rope without
  touching it.

Dilo raises the belt toward them. They don't move. They just stand at
the top of the ramp while the broadcast fades out.

**Cut to black.**

**Final shot before the WDAA logo:** Anky Bronson in a hallway, watching
the feed on a monitor. He is taping his wrists. He doesn't look up when
the show ends.

## Story threads entering Episode 3 (and the Paleozoic PPV)

1. **Anky vs. Dilo at Paleozoic PPV.** The title match we didn't know
   we were building to. The People's Tank vs. the chaos king.
2. **The two silhouettes.** Who are they? Velo speculated about "other
   pack members" once in an interview, and Dilo rejected him by name —
   so whoever the two are, they are *not* Velo. Fan theories incoming.
3. **Rex's arc.** The Apex Champion is now a former champion whose
   father won't answer his calls. Heel-to-face turn live on the table.
   Alternatively: full descent into villainy. He's CONF 6 / MOT 9 —
   dangerous but breakable.
4. **Velo as wildcard.** 0-2, publicly embarrassed, alone. MOT 10.
   This is the statline of a dino who is about to do something
   *extremely* stupid to get noticed — interfere in a PPV match,
   attack a broadcaster, challenge Dilo directly. He's now the most
   unpredictable piece on the board.
5. **The Emperor.** He is loose, humiliated, and in possession of
   information Dilo *didn't* leak. The second briefcase still hasn't
   been opened. There's a second act for him if he wants one.
6. **The Commission.** Dilo claimed he'd sent copies to "the
   Commission." The WDAA has never mentioned a Commission on air
   before. This is a new named entity and it's sitting in the chamber.
